glassworks

A lone frame occupies the wall like a quiet reservoir of shifting light. Each time a photograph enters, the machine pares it to a square shard and lets it slide among its neighbours; colours drift, seams realign, the surface breathes. When the movement settles, a brief poem appears beside the glass — cool, spare, almost whispered — then waits for the next disturbance. The work is an aquarium of memory: fragments swirl inside strict borders, never still yet never escaping, a discipline of perpetual recomposition.
